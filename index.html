popular destinations in jordan

Amman has a rich history dating back thousands of years. Originally known as "Philadelphia" in ancient times, it has been inhabited since the Neolithic period. Throughout its history, it has been influenced by various civilizations, including the Greeks, Romans, Byzantines, and Islamic Caliphates.

  • Float in the Dead Sea: One of the most iconic experiences at the Dead Sea is floating effortlessly in its buoyant waters due to its high salt content.
  • Cover Yourself in Dead Sea Mud: The mineral-rich mud found along the shores of the Dead Sea is believed to have therapeutic properties for the skin. Cover yourself in the mud, let it dry, and then rinse off in the sea.
  • Visit Masada: Masada is an ancient fortress perched on a cliff overlooking the Dead Sea. Take a cable car or hike up to the top and explore the archaeological ruins, including the palace complex and Roman siege works. The views from the top are breathtaking.

  • Hiking: Lace up your hiking boots and explore the numerous hiking trails that crisscross Wadi Rum. Trekking through the desert allows you to discover hidden oases, ancient petroglyphs, and panoramic viewpoints overlooking the vast desert expanse.
  • Rock Climbing: Wadi Rum is a world-renowned destination for rock climbing enthusiasts. The sandstone cliffs and mountains offer a wide range of climbing routes suitable for all levels, from beginners to experienced climbers. Guided climbing tours and equipment rental are available.
  • Jeep Tours: One of the most popular ways to explore Wadi Rum is by taking a jeep tour. Experienced local guides will take you on exhilarating off-road adventures through the desert, showcasing the most scenic spots, including natural arches, canyons, and sand dunes.
